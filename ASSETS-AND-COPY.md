### 01 / 08 · FORZA (`/work/forza`) — Game websites
- Eyebrows: `Forza · forza.net` / `Game websites`
- Headline: **Cars. / Community. / Creativity.** (accent line 3)
- Subhead: The web home of the Forza franchise, for Turn 10 and Playground Games. Millions of players pass through it on their way to Motorsport and Horizon, which tends to focus the mind.
- Meta: Web design & art direction · Xbox
- Facts: Client — Turn 10 · Playground Games / Platform — forza.net / Role — Web design & art direction / Output — Franchise hub & editorial system
- Reel caption: forza.net, the franchise hub.
- **01 The brief — Two series, one home.** Forza is two flagship series and one very devoted community, so the site works as a franchise hub first. News, updates, car reveals and community programmes for Motorsport and Horizon alike, and it needed to feel like Forza before a single word is read.
- Split: Speed matters twice over here. The pages had to load quickly for a global audience, and the design had to feel quick standing still, all forward lean and racing lines borrowed from the games themselves.
- Quote: "The lobby before the lobby."
- Caption: An editorial system tuned for reveals, seasons and patch notes alike.
- **02 The system — Editorial, engineered.** The answer was an editorial system rather than a launch page. Modular components handle everything from cover reveals to patch notes, with the car photography doing the heavy lifting and the typography knowing when to step aside. ¶ It had to hold up on a phone at a bus stop just as well as on an ultrawide, so the grid flexes generously and the type scale stays disciplined throughout.
- Statement: One home for two racing **franchises.**
- **03 In the wild — Racing's front door.** The site serves one of racing's biggest communities day to day, quietly flexing between seasons, launches and events. Of all the work at this scale, Forza is the one I still check in on like an old colleague.
- Credits: Client — Turn 10 Studios · Playground Games · Xbox / Role — Web design & art direction / Disciplines — Web · editorial · motion

### 02 / 08 · TURN 10 STUDIOS (`/work/turn-10`) — Studio websites
- Eyebrows: `Turn 10 Studios` / `Studio websites`
- Headline: **Speed. / Precision. / Obsession.** (accent line 3)
- Subhead: Turn 10 engineer racing games to a standard that borders on the obsessive, and their studio site deserved the same care. This is the team behind Forza, shown as themselves.
- Meta: Studio site & identity · Xbox Game Studios
- Facts: Client — Turn 10 · Xbox Game Studios / Disciplines — Brand · 3D · Motion · Web / Role — Creative direction / Output — Identity & interactive site
- Reel caption: This is Turn 10.
- **01 The brief — Built on the studio's DNA.** We started with a proper breakdown of the studio's DNA. What drives them, what makes them distinct, and what sits behind all that machinery. The sketching kept returning to the same theme, the human touch behind their high-performance technology.
- Split: The visual system took its cues from motorsport design, race telemetry and the clean, no-nonsense look of technical schematics, grounded by a forward-leaning italic that keeps everything in motion.
- Quote: "Yellow cutting through greyscale. Urgency, energy, motion."
- Caption: An interactive representation of the studio office.
- **02 The idea — The office, made interactive.** Early on we settled on representing the studio in an interactive, dynamic way, an online version of their office that shows each discipline in its natural habitat. Initial sketches were made in Photoshop, then laid out for our 3D modeller. ¶ We tested building the model in WebGL, but the fidelity expected of a leader in 3D graphics meant outputting the animations as video, a library of looped sequences triggered by interaction. ¶ Click an area of the office and you're given a flythrough and an animated lobby for each discipline. Every one needed its own content, right down to the sticky notes on the whiteboards.
- Statement: A studio shown through its **people.**
- **03 The result — Absolute focus, absolute control.** From the isometric studio map to the high-impact timeline, every element was tuned to echo Turn 10's philosophy of absolute focus and absolute control. The result is a studio presence that moves as quickly as the games it makes.
- Credits: Client — Turn 10 Studios · Xbox Game Studios / Role — Brand identity, interactive direction, UX, content / Disciplines — Brand · 3D · motion · web

- Headline: **Grim. / Dark. / Glorious.** (accent line 3)
- Subhead: Identity and creative direction for Chaos Gate: Deathwatch, with Frontier and Other Things. Forty millennia of lore, a famously devoted fanbase, and a licence that guards both closely.
- Meta: Identity & creative direction · Frontier · Other Things
- Facts: Client — Frontier Developments / Agency — Other Things / Role — Identity, logo, brand guidelines / Licence — Games Workshop · Warhammer 40,000
- Reel caption: Chaos Gate: Deathwatch.
